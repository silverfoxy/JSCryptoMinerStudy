<h1 style="background:#eeeeee; color:#cf7606; padding: 0.4em; font-weight: bold; font-size: 200%;"> <span class="mw-headline" id="Welcome_to_RepRap.org">Welcome to <span style="color:#005288;">RepRap.org</span></span></h1>
<div style="padding:0px 10px 10px;">
<div style="float: right;">

<p><br />
</p><p><font size="+2"><b>RepRap</b></font> is humanity's first general-purpose <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-replicating_machine" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Self-replicating machine">self-replicating manufacturing machine</a>.
</p><p><b>RepRap</b> takes the form of a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_software" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Free software">free</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_printing" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:3D printing">desktop 3D printer</a> capable of printing plastic objects. Since many parts of RepRap are made from plastic and RepRap prints those parts, RepRap self-replicates by making a kit of itself  - a kit that anyone can assemble given time and materials.  It also means that - if you've got a RepRap - you can print <a href="/wiki/Printable_part_sources" title="Printable part sources">lots of useful stuff</a>, and you can <a href="/mediawiki/images/5/5f/Three_mendels_parent_children.jpg" class="internal" title="Three mendels parent children.jpg">print another RepRap</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Loaner_Program" title="Loaner Program" class="mw-redirect">friend</a>...
</p><p><b>RepRap is about making self-replicating machines</b>, and making them freely available for the benefit of everyone.  We are using 3D printing to do this, but if you have other technologies that can copy themselves and that can be made freely available to all, then this is the place for you too.
</p><p><b>Reprap.org is a community project</b>, which means you are welcome to edit most pages on this site, or better yet, create new pages of your own.  Our <a href="/wiki/Community_portal" title="Community portal">community portal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Development" title="Development" class="mw-redirect">New Development</a> pages have more information on how to get involved.  Use the links below and on the left to explore the site contents. You'll find some content <a href="/wiki/Translations" title="Translations">translated into other languages</a>.
</p><p><b>RepRap</b> was the first of the low-cost 3D printers, and the RepRap Project started the open-source 3D printer revolution.  It has become the most widely-used 3D printer among the global members of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maker_culture" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Maker culture">Maker Community</a>.
</p><p><b>RepRap</b> was voted <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://3dprint.com/192706/10-significant-things-results/">the most significant 3D-printed object</a> in 2017.  That is to say people think that the most important thing you can print in a 3D printer is another 3D printer - the whole reason for the <b>RepRap</b> project.  Also in 2017 <b>RepRap'</b>s creator <a href="/wiki/User:Adrianbowyer" title="User:Adrianbowyer">Adrian Bowyer</a> was awarded the 3D Printing Industry's <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/dr-adrian-bowyer-receive-outstanding-contribution-3d-printing-award-107673/">Outstanding Contribution to 3D Printing Award</a> and was inducted into the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://tctawards.com/hall-of-fame/">3D Printing Hall of Fame</a>.
</p><p><b>RepRap</b> state-of-the-art when this page was last updated (June 2017) is well represented by the <a href="/wiki/Snappy" title="Snappy"> RepRap Snappy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dollo" title="Dollo"> RepRap Dollo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Generation_7_Electronics" title="Generation 7 Electronics">RepRap Generation 7 Electronics</a>.  By state-of-the-art we mean the best self-replicators.  These may not be the most technically advanced 3D printers, but - arguably - any self-replicator is, by that very fact, more advanced than anything that is not.
</p>
