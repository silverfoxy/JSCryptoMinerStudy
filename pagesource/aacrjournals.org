<p>Hot off the press! Read the just released <em>Cancer Discovery</em> article, <a href="http://cancerdiscovery.aacrjournals.org/content/early/2018/03/08/2159-8290.CD-17-1134?utm_source=olf&utm_medium=spotlight&utm_campaign=171134">The Pancreatic Cancer Microbiome Promotes Oncogenesis by Induction of Innate and Adaptive Immune Suppression</a>, Miller, et al.  <a href="http://www.aacr.org/Newsroom/Pages/News-Release-Detail.aspx?ItemID=1160#.WrPisajwbIU">Read the press release</a></p>

</div>

<div class="spot">
<p>Read &quot;<a href="http://cancerres.aacrjournals.org/content/72/6/1494?utm_source=mar18&amp;utm_medium=spotlight&amp;utm_campaign=113948">ARN-509: A Novel Antiandrogen for Prostate Cancer Treatment</a>&quot; by Nicola Clegg, Jeffrey Hager, and colleagues, which published in <em>Cancer Research</em> in 2012. Six years later, ARN-509, now known as apalutamide, received FDA approval for treating men with nonmetastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer. The 2012 study provides the first information about the discovery and preclinical development of this antihormone agent. Learn more by reading the <a href="http://blog.aacr.org/fda-prostate-cancer/">AACR Cancer Research Catalyst</a>.</p>
</div>
</article>

<div class="spotgraphic">
<p style="vertical-align: text-top;"><a href="http://cebp.aacrjournals.org/content/27/3/245?utm_source=mar18&amp;utm_medium=spotlight&amp;utm_campaign=170488"><img src="http://aacrjournals.org/sites/default/files/images/spotlight/CEBP-HOTP_200x111.jpg" /> </a></p>
</div>

<div class="spot">
<p>Read &quot;<a href="http://cebp.aacrjournals.org/content/27/3/245?utm_source=mar18&amp;utm_medium=spotlight&amp;utm_campaign=170488">Multilevel small-area estimation of colorectal cancer screening in the United States</a>&quot; in the March issue of <a href="http://cebp.aacrjournals.org/content/27/3?utm_source=mar18&amp;utm_medium=spotlight&amp;utm_campaign=toc&amp;current-issue=y"><em>Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers &amp; Prevention</em></a>. Berkowitz and colleagues examined colorectal cancer screening rates across the United States. While most previous analyses have estimated the prevalence of CRC screening nationally or by state, Berkowitz and colleagues estimated CRC screening rates in all U.S. counties. This detailed view enabled them to identify where CRC screening rates are the lowest and illuminate disparities across ethnicities. <a href="http://www.aacr.org/Newsroom/Pages/News-Release-Detail.aspx?ItemID=1151#.Wp6e--jwbIU">Read the press release</a>.</p>
</div>
</article>
