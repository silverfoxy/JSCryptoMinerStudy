      <td width="750" class="pdi"><p>Born from one family&rsquo;s passion for Kenya and its wilderness, the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust is today the most successful orphan-elephant rescue and rehabilitation program in the world and one of the pioneering conservation organisations for wildlife and habitat protection in East Africa.</p>

<p>Founded in 1977 by Dr Dame Daphne Sheldrick D.B.E, in honour of the memory of her late husband, famous naturalist and founding Warden of Tsavo East National Park, David Leslie William Sheldrick MBE, the DSWT claims a rich and deeply rooted family history in wildlife and conservation.</p>

<p>The DSWT has remained true to its principles and ideals, remaining a sustainable and flexible organisation. Guided by experienced and dedicated Trustees and assisted by an Advisory Committee of proactive naturalists with a lifetime of wildlife and environmental experience, the Trust takes effective action and achieves long-lasting results.</p>

<p><strong>Mission statement</strong></p>

<blockquote><em>The David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust embraces all measures that compliment the conservation, preservation and protection of wildlife. These include anti-poaching, safe guarding the natural environment, enhancing community awareness, addressing animal welfare issues, providing veterinary assistance to animals in need, rescuing and hand rearing elephant and rhino orphans, along with other species that can ultimately enjoy a quality of life in wild terms when grown.</em></blockquote>

<p>Chaired by Daphne Sheldrick, the DSWT is run by Angela Sheldrick, the daughter of David and Daphne, who has been managing all of the Trust&rsquo;s activities for over a decade. Growing up in Tsavo and later in the Nairobi National Park, Angela has been part of the Trust&rsquo;s vision from the start, supported by her husband Robert Carr-Hartley and their two boys Taru and Roan, who are passionate about Kenya&rsquo;s wildlife and eager to ensure that David and Daphne&rsquo;s legacy continues.</p>

<p>In 2004 the DSWT was incorporated as a charity in the U.K. and granted charitable status by the Charities Commission, whilst during the same year the Trust has also attained U.S. Charitable status enhancing its corporate funding capability under the guidance of the U.S. Friends of the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust.</p>

<p>Helping save the lives of orphaned elephants and rhinos through the &lsquo;<a href="asp/orphans.asp">Orphans&rsquo; Project</a>&rsquo; who are ultimately released back into the wild is just one of the many wildlife commitments the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust is involved in. The DSWT also runs 9 full-time <a href="desnaring/index_new.asp">Anti-Poaching and Desnaring Units</a>, 4 <a href="mobilevet/index_new.asp">Mobile Veterinary Units</a> and the <a href="mobilevet/skyvets.html">Sky Vets</a> initiative, 5 &lsquo;<a href="desnaring/aerialsurveillance.html">Aerial Surveillance</a>&rsquo; planes and a rapid response helicopter, whilst being active in &lsquo;<a href="html/savinghabitats.html">Saving Habitats</a>&rsquo;, &lsquo;<a href="html/conservationinitiatives.html">Conservation Initiatives</a>&rsquo; and &lsquo;<a href="asp/community_outreach.asp">Community Outreach</a>&rsquo;.</p>
