<center><h1>Welcome to Amber!</h1><nocenter>

<!-- Main page narration -->
<p><b>Amber</b> is a suite of biomolecular simulation programs.  It began in
the late 1970's,  and is
maintained by an active develpment community; see our <a
href="History.php">history page</a> and our <a href="contributors.html">
contributors page</a> for more information.</p>
<p>
<p>The term "Amber" refers to two things.  First, it is a set of molecular mechanical
<a href="AmberModels.php">force fields</a> for the simulation of biomolecules
(these force fields are in the public domain, and are used in a variety of
simulation programs).  Second, it is a <a href="AmberTools.php">package of
molecular simulation programs</a> which includes source code and demos.</p>

<p>Amber is distributed in two parts:
<a href="AmberTools.php">AmberTools17</a> and
<a href="AmberMD.php">Amber16</a>.  You can use AmberTools17 without
Amber16, but not <i>vice versa</i>.  See the <a href="GetAmber.php">Download
Amber</a> link for information on how to download the code.</p>

<p>Amber is developed in an active collaboration of <a
href="http://casegroup.rutgers.edu">David Case</a> at Rutgers
University, <a href="http://www.chpc.utah.edu/~cheatham/">Tom
Cheatham</a> at the University of Utah, <a
href="http://www.merzgroup.org">Ken Merz</a> at Michigan State
University, <a href="http://www.qtp.ufl.edu/~roitberg/">Adrian
Roitberg</a> at the University of Florida, <a
href="http://laufercenter.stonybrook.edu/simmerling/Home">Carlos
Simmerling</a> at SUNY-Stony Brook, <a
href="https://www.faculty.uci.edu/profile.cfm?faculty_id=4618">Ray Luo</a> at UC Irvine, <a
href="http://www.pharmacy.pitt.edu/directory/profile.php?profile=1639">Junmei Wang</a> at
the University of Pittsburgh, <a href="http://www.rosswalker.co.uk">Ross
Walker</a> at GSK, and <a href="contributors.html">many
others</a>.  Amber was originally developed under the leadership of Peter
Kollman.

<p><b>A good general overview of the Amber codes can be found in:</b>
</p>
<p> R. Salomon-Ferrer, D.A. Case, R.C. Walker.  An overview of the Amber
biomolecular simulation package. <i>WIREs Comput. Mol. Sci.</i> <b>3,</b>
198-210 (2013). (<a href="2012_wires.pdf">PDF</a>)
</p>
<p>
D.A. Case, T.E. Cheatham, III, T. Darden, H. Gohlke, R. Luo, K.M. Merz, Jr., A.
Onufriev, C. Simmerling, B. Wang and R. Woods.  The Amber biomolecular
simulation programs.  <i>J. Computat. Chem.</i> <b>26,</b> 1668-1688 (2005).
</p>
<p>
See the <a href="AmberModels.php">force field page</a> for information about
force fields that the code supports.
</p>
