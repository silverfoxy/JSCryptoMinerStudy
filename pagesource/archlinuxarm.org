  <div class="col-sm-4">
    <h3>Simple</h3>
    <p>We are a port of Arch Linux, which aims for simplicity and full control to the end user. We provide a light-weight base structure that allows you to shape the system to your needs.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="col-sm-4">
    <h3>Optimized</h3>
    <p>We build optimized packages for soft-float ARMv5te, hard-float ARMv6 and ARMv7, and ARMv8 AArch64 instruction sets to use each platform to its full potential.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="col-sm-4">
    <h3>Up to Date</h3>
    <p>New software versions are packaged as they are released, ensuring you are always on the leading edge of stable software releases.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<hr>

<div class="row">
  <div class="col-sm-12">
    <p>Arch Linux ARM is a distribution of Linux for ARM computers. We provide targeted kernel and software support for soft-float ARMv5te, hard-float ARMv6 and ARMv7, and ARMv8 AArch64 instruction sets on a variety of consumer devices and development platforms. Our collaboration with Arch Linux brings users the best platform, newest packages, and installation support.</p>
    <p>Arch Linux ARM carries forward the Arch Linux philosophy of simplicity and user-centrism, targeting and accommodating <i>competent</i> Linux users by giving them complete control and responsibility over the system. Instructions are provided to assist in navigating the nuances of installation on the various ARM platforms; however, the system itself will offer little assistance to the user.</p>
    <p>The entire distribution is on a rolling-release cycle that can be updated daily through small packages instead of huge updates on a defined release schedule. Most packages are unmodified from what the upstream developer originally released.</p>
  </div>
</div>
