Salem State University  Marsh Hall, Salem, Massachusetts. WagnerHodgson Landscape Architecture / image credit: Robert Benson</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Gebran-Tueni-Memorial.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2014awards/059.html"><h2>Memorials</h2></a><p>In Beirut, Lebanon, the scene of decades of violence, a new memorial by Vladimir Djurovic Landscape Architecture honors Gebran Tueni, a journalist assassinated by a car bomb. Stones etched with his name are meant to be taken, as they are continuously replenished.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2014 Professional General Design Honor Award. Gebran Tueni Memorial. Beirut Central District, Beirut, Lebanon. Vladimir Djurovic Landscape Architecture / Image credit: Matteo Piazza</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Philadelphia-Navy-Yard.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2014awards/326.html"><h2>Reuse</h2></a><p>The Navy abandoned Philadelphia's Navy Yard in the mid-1990s but left a rich legacy of infrastructure and buildings that became the headquarters for Urban Outfitters. D.I.R.T. Studio salvaged left-over materials, reworking them into something boldly new. </p><label class="bylineText">2014 ASLA Professional General Design Honor Award. Studio</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/residential-2015.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2014awards/380.html"><h2>Residential Design</h2></a><p>In Sagaponack, New York, the Atlantic Ocean is slowly encroaching due to climate change. There, LaGuardia Landscape Architects recreated the historic coastal landscape of dunes and meadows.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2013 Professional Residential Design Award of Excellence. Sagaponack Residence. Sagaponack, New York. LaGuardia Design Landscape Architects / image credit: Eva Cocco</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Tech-Town.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2014awards/055.html"><h2>Urban Revitalization</h2></a><p>Through an ambitious new plan, a multi-disciplinary team at Sasaki Associates is helping to revitalize TechTown, a knowledge district in midtown Detroit. The goal is to create a hub for innovation and collaboration through improvements to the public realm.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2014 Professional Analysis and Planning Award of Excellence. Midtown Detroit Techtown District. Detroit, Michigan. Sasaki Associates, Inc. / image credit: Sasaki Associates</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Woodland-Rain-Garden.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2014awards/602.html"><h2>Rain Gardens</h2></a><p>Caddo Parish, Louisiana, gets 5 to 6 feet of rain every year. Woodland Rain Gardens. Caddo Parish, Louisiana. To improve biodiversity, both saltwater and freshwater wetlands are being restored.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2010 Professional Analysis and Planning Honor Award. Orongo Station Conservation Master Plan. New Zealand. Nelson Byrd Woltz Landscape Architects / image credit: Nelson Byrd Woltz Landscape Architects</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Rooftop-Haven.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2010awards/377.html"><h2>Green Roofs</h2></a><p>This green roof in Chicago not only cools the air and absorbs stormwater, but also produces 1,000 pounds of organic food annually. Hoerr Schaudt Landscape Architects designed this haven for the Gary Comer Youth Center to also be a learning environment.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2010 Professional General Design Honor Award. Rooftop Haven for Urban Agriculture, Chicago, Illinois. Hoerr Schaudt Landscape Architects / image credit: Scott Shigley</label></div></li><li><img src="/uploadedImages/CMS/HomePage_New/Lafitte-Corridor.jpg"></img><div class="uk-overlay-panel uk-overlay-background"><a href="https://www.asla.org/2013awards/328.html"><h2>Transportation</h2></a><p>In New Orleans, the 3-mile-long Lafitte Greenway, covering some 50 acres of barren land, will become a sustainable transportation corridor. Landscape architecture firm Design Workshop worked with the city government to fully envision a linear green park.</p><label class="bylineText">ASLA 2013 Professional Analysis and Planning Award of Excellence. Lafitte Greenway + Revitalization Corridor. New Orleans, Louisiana. Design Workshop, Inc. / image credit: Design Workshop, Inc.</label></div></li></ul>
