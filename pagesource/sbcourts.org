	              <div class="content2" id="Page_1">
	                <p> <a href="dv/appeals.shtm">Appeals</a><br />
	                  <br />
	                  A non-prevailing party in a case may ask a higher court to review the decision in their case and determine if the decision was correct. </p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_2">
	                <p><a href="dv/civil.shtm">Civil</a><br />
	                  <br />
	                  A civil matter involves a lawsuit in which one party sues another to recover money, real property or personal property, to enforce a contract or an obligation, to co/llect damages for injury or to protect some civil right. </p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_3">
	                <p><a href="dv/criminal.shtm">Criminal</a> <br />
	                  <br />
	                  Criminal cases range from relatively minor offenses such as traffic infractions to serious ones such as robbery and murder.</p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_4">
	                <p><a href="dv/fl/index.shtm">Family Law</a> <br />
	                  <br />
	                  Superior Court has programs that provide limited services in family law: Family Court Services and Family Law Facilitator </p>
	                <ul>
	                  <li><a href="dv/fl/LITIGANTS/">General Information for Family Law Litigants</a></li>
	                  </ul>
                  </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_5">
	                <p><a href="dv/juvenile.shtm">Juvenile</a> <br />
	                  <br />
	                  In California, as in all states, there is a separate court system for persons under the age of 18, who are known as minors or juveniles. The juvenile courts have the ability to intervene in three different types of circumstances: Delinquency, Status Offenders, Dependent Children. </p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_8">
	                <p><a href="dv/small-claims.shtm">Small Claims</a> <br />
	                  <br />
	                  Small Claims is a court where you can exercise your rights to resolve a dispute that do not exceed $10,000 and are resolved quickly and inexpensively with relatively simple and informal rules. </p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_9">
	                <p><a href="dv/traffic-minor-offenses.shtm">Traffic/Minor Offenses</a> <br />
	                  <br />
	                  Violations of traffic laws, municipal code violations and county ordinances are heard in Traffic <a href="dv/traffic-minor-offenses.shtm">Court.</a></p>
	                </div>
	              <div class="content2" id="Page_11">
	                <p><a href="dv/jury-service.shtm">Jury Services </a><br />
	                  <br />
	                  The right to trial by  a jury is the privilege of every person in the United States.&nbsp; This right  is guaranteed by the U.S. Constitution.&nbsp; Jurors are essential to the  administration of justice.</p>
	                </div>
