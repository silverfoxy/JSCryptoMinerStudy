                    <div class="tab-process-content first-tab active">
                      <h2 class="nojs">Internet-Drafts</h2>
                      <p>Internet-Drafts are working documents of the IETF, its areas, and its working groups. During the development of a specification, draft versions of the document are made available for informal review and comment by placing them in the IETF&#39;s Internet-Draft format.</p>
                      <ul class="actions">
                          
                            <li><a class="default" href="/standards/process/">Find out more about the IETF standards process</a></li>
                          
                        </ul>
                    </div>
                    <!-- Second tab content -->
                    <div class="tab-process-content second-tab">
                      <h2 class="nojs">Request for Comments (RFC<span style="text-transform: lowercase">s</span>)</h2>
                      <p>The IETF publishes RFCs authored by network operators, engineers and computer scientists to document methods, behaviors, research, or innovations applicable to the Internet.</p>
                      <ul class="actions">
                          
                            <li><a class="default" href="/standards/rfcs/">Learn more about RFCs</a></li>
                          
                        </ul>
                    </div>
                    <!-- third tab content -->
                    <div class="tab-process-content third-tab">
                      <h2 class="nojs">Working groups</h2>
                      <p>Working Groups are the primary mechanism for development of IETF specifications and guidelines. Working Groups are typically created to address a specific problem or to produce one or more specific deliverables (a guideline, standards specification, etc.).</p>
                      <ul class="actions">
                          
                            <li><a class="default" href="/how/wgs/">See how IETF Working Groups operate</a></li>
                          
                        </ul>

                <ul class="topics-teaser three-col no-margin-bottom">
                  
                    <li>
                        <a href="/topics/netmgmt/">

                        <h2>Automated network management</h2>
                        <p>The IETF is working on standards for automated network management which, as the name implies aims to improve and make more efficient management of networks as they continue increase in size and complexity.</p>
                        </a>
                    </li>
                  
                    <li>
                        <a href="/topics/iot/">
                        
                          <div class="img-holder">
                            
                            <img src="/media/images/things.max-300x300_CJcb1xp.jpg" alt="things">
                          </div>
                        
                        <h2>The Internet of Things</h2>
                        <p>The Internet of Things is the network of physical objects or &quot;things&quot; embedded with electronics, software, sensors, and connectivity to enable objects to exchange data with the manufacturer, operator and/or other connected devices.</p>
                        </a>
                    </li>
                  
                    <li>
                        <a href="/topics/transport/">
                        
                          <div class="img-holder">
                            
                            <img src="/media/images/quicpic2.max-300x300.jpg" alt="Quicpic2">
                          </div>
                        
                        <h2>New transport technology</h2>
                        <p>The development of new transport technologies in the IETF provide capabilities that improve the ability of Internet applications to send data over the Internet.</p>
