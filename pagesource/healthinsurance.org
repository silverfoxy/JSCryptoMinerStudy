				<h1>Your guide to buying individual health insurance</h1>
				<h2>Thanks to special enrollment periods, millions of Americans can still buy ACA-compliant plans.  </h2>
			</header>

			<div class="columns">

				<div class="column wide">
					<header>

<ul class="post_author">
	<li class="author_name"><a href="/authors/louise-norris/">Louise Norris</a></li>
	<li>Individual health insurance and health reform authority; broker</li>
	<li>February 5, 2018 </li>
</ul>

					<div class="full_text" itemprop="articleBody">
						
<h3>2018 Obamacare enrollment</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="#sep">Open enrollment has ended, but consumers can still enroll if they are eligible for a special enrollment period</a>.</li>
<li><a href="#reasons">Consumers are still required to have ACA-compliant coverage.</a></li>
<li><a href="#subsidies">Premium and cost-sharing subsidies are still available</a>.</li>
<li><a href="#affordable">Exchange plans are key to affordability</a>.</li>
<li><strong>Did you miss open enrollment?</strong> <a href="#alternatives">There&#8217;s a wide range of short-term coverage that could provide a temporary safety net</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>With all of the hype about December 15 being the &#8220;last day of open enrollment,&#8221; it&#8217;s easy to understand why many Americans believe their opportunity to enroll in <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/faqs/ive-heard-that-i-need-to-have-an-aca-compliant-health-plan-but-what-does-that-mean/">ACA-compliant</a> coverage has now passed. But for millions, that&#8217;s simply not the case.</p>
<h3>Millions can buy outside of open enrollment</h3>
<p><a name="sep"></a>One obvious example: a special enrollment period – <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/blog/2017/08/25/latest-update-on-bare-counties/">in more than 20 states</a> – for consumers who had an individual market plan but <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/involuntary-loss-of-coverage-is-a-qualifying-event/">lost it because their insurer is left the marketplace</a>. Those policyholders are eligible for an SEP that ends March 1, 2018. (In states that run their own exchanges, this SEP may not be available for on-exchange plans if the exchange mapped the enrollees to a new plan for 2018.)</p>

<div class="hio_cta"><a href="/quotes/" data-gacategory="HIO CTA Shortcode Clicks" data-gaevent="%2Fquotes%2F" data-galabel="Compare+plans+and+rates+in+your+ZIP+code">Compare plans and rates in your ZIP code</a></div><!-- hio_cta -->

<p>And that special enrollment period – when <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/involuntary-loss-of-coverage-is-a-qualifying-event/">involuntary loss of coverage is the qualifying event</a> – is just one of a fairly long list of opportunities to enroll in <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/faqs/ive-heard-that-i-need-to-have-an-aca-compliant-health-plan-but-what-does-that-mean/">ACA-compliant coverage</a> outside of open enrollment. So before you give up hope on compliant coverage – and coverage that may very well include a subsidy – you should review our <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/why-you-need-this-book/">Insider&#8217;s Guide to Obamacare&#8217;s Special Enrollment</a>.</p>
<p>The list of qualifying events that could result in a special enrollment period for you at any point during the year including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/how-your-big-move-can-trigger-an-sep/">A permanent move</a> (in most cases, you must have also had coverage before your move)</li>
<li><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/an-sep-for-your-growing-family/">Marriage or the birth or adoption of a child</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/non-calendar-year-renewal-as-a-qualifying-event/">Your non-calendar-year plan reaches its renewal date</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/a-change-in-subsidy-eligibility-changes-your-options/">A change in your ACA subsidy eligibility</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/citizenship-status-can-deliver-coverage/">A change in your citizenship status</a></li>
<li>and <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/special-enrollment-guide/why-you-need-this-book/">more</a></li>
</ul>
<p>
<div class="hio_cta"><a href="/quotes/" data-gacategory="HIO CTA Shortcode Clicks" data-gaevent="%2Fquotes%2F" data-galabel="If+you%27re+eligible+for+a+special+enrollment+period%2C+you+can+still+shop+for+ACA-compliant+plans.+Compare+plans+and+premiums+here+by+ZIP+code.">If you're eligible for a special enrollment period, you can still shop for ACA-compliant plans. Compare plans and premiums here by ZIP code.</a></div><!-- hio_cta -->
<br />
<a name="reasons"></a></p>
<h3>Your reasons to enroll <em>haven&#8217;t</em> changed</h3>
<div id="attachment_37822" style="max-width: 410px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/blog/2018/02/07/enrolling-like-their-lives-depend-on-it/"><img class="wp-image-37822 size-thumbnail" src="https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-400x209.jpg" alt="Katya Powder" width="400" height="209" srcset="https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-400x209.jpg 400w, https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-800x418.jpg 800w, https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-768x402.jpg 768w, https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-1400x732.jpg 1400w, https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder-660x345.jpg 660w, https://www.healthinsurance.org/assets/2018/01/katya-powder.jpg 1560w" sizes="(max-width: 400px) 100vw, 400px" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Read: Enrolling like their lives depend on it</p></div>
<p>If you&#8217;re wondering whether it still makes sense to enroll, consider this:</p>
<ul>
<li>You&#8217;re <em>still</em> required by law to have <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/faqs/ive-heard-that-i-need-to-have-an-aca-compliant-health-plan-but-what-does-that-mean/">ACA-compliant health coverage</a>. If you don&#8217;t, you could face the <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/glossary/individual-mandate/">individual mandate</a> penalty. Yes, the GOP tax bill included repeal of the individual mandate penalty, but that doesn&#8217;t take effect until 2019. <em>People who are uninsured in 2018 will still face a penalty</em>. <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/obamacare-penalty-calculator/">Calculate your penalty</a>.</li>
<li>Coverage is <em>still</em> <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/glossary/guaranteed-issue/">guaranteed-issue</a>, regardless of <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/blog/2015/08/19/why-pre-existing-conditions-mattered-to-millions/">pre-existing conditions</a>.</li>
<li>Premiums for older enrollees are <em>still</em> capped at no more than three times the premiums for younger enrollees.</li>
<li>All new major medical plans are <em>still</em> required to cover <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/essential-health-benefits/">essential health benefits</a>.</li>
<li>And the financial assistance provided by the ACA is still available, with <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/10-reasons-cost-sharing-reductions-are-yuge-news/#8">premium subsidies that are larger than ever in most areas of the country</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/blog/2018/02/07/enrolling-like-their-lives-depend-on-it/">Four Americans who are still enrolling – like their live depend on it</a><br />
<a name="subsidies"></a></p>
<h3>Subsidies can still greatly reduce your plan costs</h3>
<p>Depending on your household income, you may still be eligible for premium tax credits (aka, <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/will-you-receive-an-obamacare-premium-subsidy/">premium subsidies</a>) and possibly <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/glossary/cost-sharing-reductions/">cost-sharing reductions</a> (CSR, aka, cost-sharing subsidies).</p>
<ul>
<li>Premiums subsidies are still available in the exchange for people with income up to 400 percent of the poverty level. (For 2018 coverage, a single person can earn up to $48,240 and be eligible for the premium tax credit, and a family of four can earn up to $98,400). <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/subsidy-calculator/">Calculate your subsidy</a>. In 2017, 84 percent of exchange enrollees received premium subsidies that covered an average of two-thirds of the total premiums.</li>
<li>The only thing that has changed about premium subsidies for 2018 is that they&#8217;re larger than they were in 2017 in most areas, since average premiums increased and the subsidies grow to keep up with premiums.</li>
<li>Cost-sharing subsidies are also still available in 2018, even though the Trump Administration has eliminated <em>funding</em> for cost-sharing reductions. People with income up to 250 percent of the <a href="http://federal poverty">federal poverty level</a> still have access to Silver plans with reduced out-of-pocket costs.</li>
</ul>
<p><a name="affordable"></a></p>
<h3>A few reminders about affordable coverage</h3>
<p>Given the premium increases that took effect in most areas for 2018, it’s essential for anyone who is eligible for premium tax credits – or who might be eligible with an income fluctuation later in the year – to enroll through the exchange if and when they have a special enrollment period. Don’t sign up for an <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/obamacare/off-exchange-plans/">off-exchange</a> plan and miss out on the possibility of much more affordable premiums via a tax credit.</p>
<p>In general, the only people who should be enrolling off-exchange are those who are 100 percent certain that there is no way they will qualify for a premium tax credit during the year – keeping in mind that the premium tax credits are available well into the middle class and are larger in 2018 than they were in 2017 in order to offset the higher premiums. (A family of four earning $98,400 is eligible for premium subsidies in 2018.)</p>
<p>Remember that you have an option to either have the premium tax credit paid directly to your insurer each month to offset the amount you have to pay in premiums, <em>or</em> you can pay full price for your coverage each month and claim the full premium tax credit when you file your tax return.</p>
<p>But either way, it&#8217;s only available if you enroll in a plan through the exchange. If you buy your plan off-exchange, there&#8217;s no way to claim the tax credit at the end of the year, even if your income ultimately ends up at a level that would have been subsidy-eligible.<br />
<a name="alternatives"></a></p>
<h3>Short-term and other Obamacare alternatives</h3>
<p>We’re big fans of the Affordable Care Act and what it’s done to improve access to affordable coverage. We know that the law has delivered health insurance for millions who were unable to find affordable coverage on the individual market in the past. And – if at all possible – we strongly encourage our readers to take advantage of the comprehensive ACA-compliant coverage the law has provided.</p>
<p>At the same time, we do recognize that there is a segment of the individual market population that is facing daunting rate increases. We realize that their coverage options may be limited – at least for 2018 coverage.</p>
<p>To those consumers facing high premiums – perhaps because they’re in the <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/faqs/what-is-the-medicaid-coverage-gap-and-who-does-it-affect/">coverage gap</a> or their incomes make them ineligible for subsidies – we still would emphasize that some health coverage is always better than no coverage. The good news: there’s a wide range of <a href="https://www.healthinsurance.org/short-term-health-insurance/">short-term health coverage</a> available that could provide a temporary safety net until you can find less expensive comprehensive coverage.</p>

Can't afford ACA-compliant coverage? Get up to 360 days of short-term coverage for as little as $50 a month.</a></div><!-- hio_cta -->
